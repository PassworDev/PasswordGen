Password Protection & Online Safety

Introduction to Password Safety

Passwords are like keys to your online world. Every account—email, social media, gaming platforms, school accounts—needs a password. Just like you wouldn’t leave your house unlocked, don’t leave accounts unprotected.

Example: Someone logging into your social media and sending messages pretending to be you could hurt friendships or get you into trouble.
Activity: List all the accounts you use online. Check if passwords are weak or repeated.

Risks of Weak Passwords

  • 123456
  • password
  • qwerty
  • your name or birthday
Tip: Avoid using the same password for multiple accounts.
Example: A social media influencer was hacked because of a weak password, causing embarrassment.

Characteristics of a Strong Password

  • Length: At least 12 characters
  • Complexity: Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols
  • Uniqueness: Avoid personal info like name or birthday
Weak: dog123 | Strong: D0g!Run$Fast12

Creating Memorable but Strong Passwords

  • Use passphrases: Combine random words, e.g., BlueCatsRun7Fast!
  • Letter & symbol substitution: E → 3, A → @, I → 1
  • Avoid repeating passwords
Mini activity: Create a password using three unrelated words + numbers + symbol.

Password Managers

  • Store all passwords securely
  • Generate strong passwords automatically
  • Fill passwords safely on websites
Example: Remember one master password; app stores the rest.
Tip: Keep your master password strong and never share it.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

  • SMS code
  • Authenticator apps
  • Email verification
Tip: 2FA adds a second lock even if password is compromised.

Staying Safe Online

  • Never share passwords
  • Don’t save passwords on public/shared computers
  • Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive accounts
  • Look for HTTPS
Scenario: Logging into a public library computer? Always log out and avoid saving passwords.

Recognizing Phishing & Scams

  • Emails claiming "Account will be closed"
  • Fake links
  • Requests for password or credit card
Activity: Check your email for suspicious messages and note why.

What to Do if Your Account is Hacked

  • Change password immediately
  • Enable 2FA
  • Check connected apps
  • Alert the platform
  • Tell a trusted adult
Example: Student’s school account hacked → changed password, enabled 2FA, reported to IT → recovered safely.

Protecting Personal Information

  • Don’t share full name, address, school, birthday
  • Don’t share passwords, PINs, codes
  • Think before sharing photos or locations
Scenario: Posting full name and address publicly allows strangers to track you.

Regular Maintenance

  • Change passwords every 3–6 months
  • Review accounts for suspicious activity
  • Delete unused accounts
  • Backup important data securely

Summary & Checklist

  • Strong, unique passwords for all accounts
  • 2FA enabled
  • Never share passwords
  • Watch for phishing emails or scams
  • Know what to do if hacked
  • Keep personal info private
  • Review accounts regularly
Your password is the key to your digital life. Stay aware and act quickly if something goes wrong.
